Derivation of $ {c^2 \over h^2} = 2 + {p \over q} + {q \over p} $

$$ {p \over a} = {h \over b}, \quad {q \over b} = {h \over a} $$ $$ \Rightarrow p = h \cdot {a \over b}, \quad q = h \cdot {b \over a} $$ $$ \Rightarrow {p \over q} = {a^2 \over b^2}, \quad {q \over p} = {b^2 \over a^2} $$ $$ $$ $$ c = p + q = h \cdot ({a \over b} + {b \over a}) $$ $$ \Rightarrow c^2 = h^2 \cdot ({a \over b} + {b \over a})^2 $$ $$ \Rightarrow {c^2 \over h^2} = {a^2 \over b^2} + 2 + {b^2 \over a^2} $$ $$ = 2 + {p \over q} + {q \over p} $$
